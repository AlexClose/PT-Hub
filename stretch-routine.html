Lower Body Stretch Routine

Perform at the end of every training session · ~10 minutes
Hold each stretch for 30 seconds per side. Breathe deeply and relax into the stretch — never bounce or force range of motion. For unilateral stretches, complete both sides before moving on.
  1. 1
    Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
    Drop to a half-kneeling position, back knee on the ground. Drive your hips forward gently until you feel a stretch in the front of the back hip. Keep your torso upright, core lightly braced. For a deeper stretch, raise the arm on the same side as the back knee overhead and lean slightly away.
    30 seceach side
  2. 2
    Kneeling Hamstring Stretch
    From the half-kneeling position, extend the front leg straight with the heel on the floor and toes pointed up. Hinge forward at the hip and reach both hands toward your toe. Keep your back flat — don't round the lower back. Hold and breathe.
    30 seceach side
  3. 3
    Butterfly Stretch
    Sit on the floor and bring the soles of your feet together, letting your knees fall out to the sides. Hold your feet with both hands and gently press your knees toward the floor with your elbows. Sit tall — don't round your back. To deepen, lean forward slightly from the hips.
    30 secboth sides
  4. 4
    Seated Toe Reach
    Sit on the floor with both legs straight out in front of you. Flex your feet (toes toward you) and reach both hands toward your toes as far as comfortable. Keep your legs as straight as possible and focus on hinging from the hips rather than rounding the upper back.
    30 secboth sides
  5. 5
    Standing Quad Pull
    Stand tall and hold onto a wall or pole for balance. Bend one knee and pull your foot toward your glute, keeping your knees together and your hips level. Stand tall — avoid leaning forward or letting the hip flare out. Focus on feeling the stretch in the front of the thigh.
    30 seceach side
  6. 6
    Wall Ankle Stretch
    Stand facing a wall. Place one foot a few inches from the wall and drive your knee forward toward the wall, keeping your heel flat on the floor. Slide the foot back until your heel just starts to lift — that's your starting position. Hold, then try to inch the foot back further each set. Switch sides.
    30 seceach side
